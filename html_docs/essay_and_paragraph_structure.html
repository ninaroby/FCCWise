Essay and Paragraph Structure

Essay Structure

    1. Introduction Paragraph
  • Move from the thesis’s general idea to the thesis itself.
    • 2. Define-the-Thesis Paragraph
  • Describe the basic idea of the thesis
  • If breaking down the thesis’s idea into sections, make sure you list the ideas toward the end of the paragraph
    • 3. Define-Sections Paragraph
  • If your thesis was broke down into sections, the following paragraphs should cover each section
    • 4. Conclusion Paragraph
  • Summarize the thesis with its sections
  • Include afterthought
  • Afterthought should be done in the third person voice (it, one, he, she, or they), depending on the nature of the paper
  • Stay away from using the first person voice (I, me, us, we) and the second person voice (you, you all)

      • Paragraph Structure:

      This structure applies namely to points 2 and 3 above
        1. Main-Idea Sentence
      • State the main idea of the paragraph
      • Define-the-Main-Idea Sentence
      • Try to make sense of the main idea
      • If necessary, use a citation to further make sense of the main idea or to support it
        • 3. Restate/Paraphrase-the-Citation Sentence
      • If the citation uses fancy language, re-write it in your own words (“In other words…”)
        • 4. Example Sentence
      • Use a valid example of the main idea
        • 5. Conclusion/Transitional-Statement Sentence
      • State the point of the main idea and hint at the next paragraph’s main idea
