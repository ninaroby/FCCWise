Present Participle and Gerund Phrases

I. Learn

Most present participle and all gerund phrases begin with an –ing word. This can cause some confusion if a tutee asks you if walking, eating, etc. are verbs. The function of present particles and gerunds is what sets them apart.

Present Participle Phrases:

  • Act as adjectives
  • Usually include objects and or modifiers that complete the thought.
    Example Sentence: The horse trotting up to the fence hopes that you have some food. Gerund Phrases:
  • Act as the noun

    • Walking on the beach is dangerous if there are thousands of jellyfish.


    What’s the verb? Walking on the beach is the subject of the verb is.
